# Invariant-Core - Closure

---

## Definition

Closure is the condition where no invalid state can survive, propagate, bypass enforcement, or re-enter the system.

More formally:

> **Closure is the absence of invalid survival paths within an enforced constraint field.**

Closure is not the absence of imaginable invalidity. It is the enforced inability of invalidity to persist inside the system.

---

## Core Rule

A system is closed when every possible invalid path is blocked, eliminated, or collapsed into invariant-preserving form.

Closure requires:

* complete constraint enforcement
* sealed boundaries
* resolved pressure
* eliminated invalidity
* no invalid recursive re-entry
* no hidden contradiction or leakage

---

## Relation To Pressure

Pressure is unresolved possibility under constraint.
Closure increases when pressure is resolved or eliminated without leakage.

Pressure prevents closure when it remains unresolved, hides inside ambiguity, leaks across a boundary, or re-enters through recursion.

---

## Relation To Invalidity

Invalidity is failure to survive constraint enforcement.
Closure is achieved when invalidity has no survival path.

A system is not closed while invalidity can:

* persist locally
* propagate globally
* cross a weak seal
* leak through an exit
* re-enter after rejection
* remain latent beneath apparent consistency

---

## Relation To Truth

Truth is what remains inside closure after invalidity is eliminated.

Closure defines the enforced boundary of truth: if invalidity can still survive, truth has not fully closed. If all invalid survival paths are sealed, eliminated, or collapsed, the remaining invariant-preserving states define truth.

---

## Boundary Behavior

Closure depends on boundary enforcement.

Every boundary must determine whether crossing states are admissible. A weak boundary preserves unresolved burden and allows leakage. A strong boundary forces resolution, elimination, or valid transformation.

Closure fails wherever a boundary permits invalidity to pass as if it were valid.

---

## Layer Behavior

Closure must hold across the full invariant stack.

A higher layer cannot declare closure while a lower layer still contains contradiction, impossibility, incoherence, or invalidity. Lower-layer violations keep the system open regardless of higher-layer acceptance.

As layers descend, closure becomes stricter because fewer forms of unresolved possibility can be deferred.

---

## Operational Test

A system is closed only when all of the following hold:

* all relevant constraints are enforced
* all boundaries are sealed against invalid passage
* all unresolved pressure has resolved, been eliminated, or been explicitly contained
* no invalid state can propagate
* no invalid state can re-enter recursively
* no apparent consistency hides latent invalidity
* no lower-layer invariant remains violated

---

## Final Compression

> **Closure = absence of invalid survival paths.**

> **Closure is reached when invalidity has nowhere left to persist, propagate, leak, or return.**
