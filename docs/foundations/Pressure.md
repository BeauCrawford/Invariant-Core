# Invariant-Core - Pressure

---

## Definition

Pressure is unresolved possibility under constraint.

More formally:

> **Pressure is the distributed burden of states, interpretations, transitions, or configurations that have not yet been validated, eliminated, or collapsed into invariant-preserving form.**

Pressure is not force, error, risk, ambiguity, uncertainty, or contradiction by itself. It can contain any of them, but its defining feature is unresolved admissibility.

Pressure exists in the gap between:

* what remains possible
* what is actually admissible

---

## Core Rule

Pressure exists only where possibility and constraint overlap without full resolution.

It requires:

* a state space
* a constraint field
* a distinction between valid and invalid
* at least one unresolved state, interpretation, transition, or configuration

Without constraints, nothing must justify itself.
Without unresolved possibility, everything has already settled.

---

## Resolution

Pressure can leave the system only by one of three paths:

* **validation** - the state satisfies the active constraints
* **elimination** - the state is rejected as invalid
* **leakage** - the burden moves elsewhere without resolution

Resolved pressure disappears.
Leaked pressure survives in relocated form.

Local calm is therefore not proof of health. Pressure may have resolved, been eliminated, or merely moved to another layer, component, abstraction, interpretation, or future state.

---

## Composition

Pressure is unresolved state burden. It commonly appears as:

* ambiguous meanings
* incompatible interpretations
* competing candidate states
* incomplete proofs
* underconstrained decisions
* latent contradictions
* unstable compositions
* unvalidated inputs
* deferred consequences
* recursive drift

The common structure is always the same: something is being carried before compatibility with invariants has been proven.

---

## Field Properties

Pressure is a field quantity, not a single event.

It has:

* **location** - where unresolved burden exists
* **magnitude** - how much burden exists
* **density** - how concentrated the burden is
* **directionality** - where it tends to move
* **gradient** - how sharply burden changes across layers or boundaries
* **accumulation** - how much burden is building over time
* **release conditions** - how it can resolve or exit
* **containment conditions** - what prevents leakage

Pressure tends to flow toward weaker seals, wider exits, less constrained layers, looser interpretations, or future states where consequence can be deferred.

---

## Primary Forms

* **Semantic pressure** - meanings are not uniquely constrained
* **Logical pressure** - assumptions or derivations are not reconciled
* **Mathematical pressure** - formal relations are incomplete, unstable, or unsettled
* **Ontological pressure** - existence conditions or categories are unresolved
* **Domain pressure** - boundaries, abstractions, or mappings are unstable
* **Architectural pressure** - structural composition has unresolved tension
* **Protocol pressure** - interaction admissibility is not fully settled
* **Algorithmic pressure** - search, convergence, or objective resolution is unsettled
* **Implementation pressure** - runtime states or code paths are underconstrained
* **Governance pressure** - decisions, policy, or responsibility remain ambiguous

These are forms of the same quantity: unresolved admissibility burden.

---

## Boundary Behavior

Seals regulate pressure at boundaries.

A seal can:

* contain pressure
* transform pressure
* force validation
* route elimination
* leak pressure forward

Strong seals may raise local pressure because they force unresolved burden to face constraints. Weak seals may lower visible local pressure while preserving global burden.

---

## Accumulation And Compression

Pressure accumulates when unresolved burden enters faster than the system validates or eliminates it.

Common causes:

* repeated deferral
* recursive re-entry
* permissive boundaries
* underconstrained interfaces
* contradictory interpretations
* weak validation
* governance ambiguity

Pressure compresses when the admissible state space narrows while unresolved burden remains. Deeper layers often make pressure denser because they allow fewer disguises and fewer deferrals.

---

## Recursion

Recursion amplifies pressure when unresolved burden can re-enter without being sealed.

Under weak sealing, recursion can preserve, hide, distribute, or magnify pressure.
Under strong sealing, recursion can refine pressure because each pass reduces invalid survivability.

---

## Stability

Pressure is not the opposite of stability.

A system can show high pressure and still be healthy if seals are strong, exits are valid, and resolution is occurring.

A system can show low pressure and still be unhealthy if burden is leaking, ambiguity is tolerated, or contradictions are displaced.

The relevant distinction is:

* **resolved pressure** strengthens stability
* **leaked pressure** weakens stability

---

## Truth

Pressure is the precursor field of truth selection.

Truth is what survives constraint enforcement after invalidity is eliminated. Pressure is the unresolved region that has not yet completed that passage.

---

## Stack Transformation

As pressure descends the invariant stack, it changes form:

| Layer | Pressure Becomes |
| ----- | ---------------- |
| Governance | process consequence |
| Implementation | runtime failure or valid execution |
| Algorithmic | convergence or non-convergence |
| Protocol | acceptance or rejection |
| Architectural | compatibility or incompatibility |
| Domain | model validity or invalidity |
| Physical / Ontological | realizability or impossibility |
| Mathematical | proof or contradiction |
| Semantic | coherent meaning or incoherence |
| Meta-Logical | consistency or collapse |

The form changes, but the underlying quantity remains unresolved admissibility burden.

---

## Operational Test

A system is carrying pressure if it must still carry any of the following before closure can increase:

* unresolved meanings
* unresolved transitions
* unresolved interactions
* unresolved candidate solutions
* deferred validation
* contradiction risk
* unproven admissibility

---

## Compressed Properties

Pressure is:

* unresolved
* constraint-relative
* field-distributed
* boundary-sensitive
* layer-transforming
* recursively amplifiable or reducible
* conserved until resolved, eliminated, or leaked
* convertible into consequence
* compressed by deeper necessity
* diagnostic of incomplete closure

---

## Final Compression

> **Pressure = unresolved possibility under constraint.**

> **Pressure is what remains to be decided before truth can close.**
